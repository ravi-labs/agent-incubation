Plan Design
Optimizer Agent

"Continuously and dynamically models optimal plan design for every sponsor — something that has never been possible to do at scale."

The problem today
Before

Decisions based on industry benchmarks, not your data

Plan sponsors make design decisions — auto-enrollment defaults, match formulas, investment menus — largely based on consultant recommendations and industry benchmarks. The analysis is generic, infrequent, and rarely tailored to a specific employer's workforce. A mid-size manufacturing company gets the same advice as a tech firm with a completely different income and age distribution.

After

Optimized recommendations built from their own workforce data

The agent analyzes the sponsor's actual participant population, models the projected impact of design changes, and delivers a prioritized recommendation with projected retirement income outcomes — specific enough for a relationship manager to take directly into a client conversation.

What the agent analyzes
👥

Participant demographics

Age distribution, income bands, tenure cohorts, and proximity to retirement — the workforce profile that makes every plan unique and every generic benchmark potentially wrong.

📊

Contribution behavior patterns

Who is contributing, at what rate, who stopped, who never started, and which cohorts are systematically under-saving relative to their income and age.

📉

Participation gaps

Which employee segments have the lowest participation rates, and what plan design levers — auto-enrollment, match structure, default rate — are most likely to move them.

🔮

Impact modeling for design changes

Runs scenario models across auto-enrollment rate, match formula, default fund, and catch-up contribution settings — projecting the change in average income replacement for each configuration.
