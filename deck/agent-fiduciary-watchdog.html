Fiduciary Watchdog
Agent

"Real-time fiduciary oversight at scale — something that is currently impossible without this agent."

The problem today
Before

Expensive, manual, and episodic

Fiduciary compliance for plan sponsors is currently handled by consultants who review things quarterly — or worse, annually. Drift can accumulate for months before anyone notices. When a finding is surfaced, a compliance officer spends hours drafting the memo. The process is slow, costly, and entirely backward-looking.

After

Continuous, autonomous, and instant

The agent runs 24/7 against every monitored plan. The moment an investment drifts out of bounds, a fee becomes unreasonable, or a participation rate drops below threshold, the agent surfaces a plain-language finding with a recommended remediation — in minutes, not months.

What the agent monitors
📉

Investment lineup vs. benchmarks

Continuously compares each fund in the lineup against performance benchmarks, peer funds, and expense ratio standards. Flags underperformers and better alternatives.

💰

Fee reasonableness

Monitors plan fees against DOL guidance and industry comparables. Surfaces cases where participants may be paying materially above-market costs, creating fiduciary exposure.

👥

Participation & coverage rates

Tracks enrollment, contribution, and coverage rates against ERISA non-discrimination thresholds. Alerts before a plan falls out of compliance with DOL requirements.

📋

Finding & remediation drafting

When a threshold is breached, automatically drafts the plain-language compliance memo — summary of the finding, regulatory basis, and recommended remediation steps.

🛡️

Policy layer: the agent knows what it can and cannot decide

Fiduciary decisions carry real legal weight. The Policy layer is configured in natural language to define exactly what the agent can surface autonomously vs. what must be escalated to a human compliance officer. The agent monitors and drafts — but certain classes of findings (e.g., fund removal recommendations, plan termination risk) are always routed for human sign-off before any communication leaves the system.

⚠ Fee Reasonableness — Medium Severity
Large-Cap Blend Fund Expense Ratio Above Threshold

The plan's large-cap blend option (Fund XYZ) has an expense ratio of 0.82%, compared to a median of 0.31% for comparable institutional share classes. Over a 20-year horizon, this differential represents an estimated $4,200 in excess costs per $100,000 invested — a material impact on participant outcomes.

Recommended remediation: Request a share class review from the fund provider, or evaluate replacing with a lower-cost comparable. A substitution analysis is available on request. This finding should be reviewed by the plan's fiduciary committee at the next meeting and documented in the ERISA §404(c) records.
